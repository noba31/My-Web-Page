Projects

1. Android Projects:

  • Pedometer

    'Pedometer' is an app that shows the number of steps and distance covered in one event, using the accelerometer of the phone.

  • Illuminating India

    'Illuminating India' is an app that provides details of places of interest, transportation, accommodation, local cuisines, hospitals, police stations and the nearest consulate, for the top five tourist places in India.

  • BioInf

    'BioInf' is an app that helps find the complement of a DNA sequence, DNA to RNA conversion and locating the 'conserved' regions between two DNA sequences.

2. Machine Learning Projects:

  • Keystroke Biometrics for Secure Banking

    - Implemented the IEEE paper: Kevin S. Killourhy, Roy A. Maxion, "Comparing Anomaly-Detection Algorithms for Keystroke Dynamics, IEEE conference on Dependable Systems & Networks, July 2009.

    - Three learning models (One Class SVM, Manhattan Distance, Gaussian Mixture Model) to predict impostors and genuine users, based on the CMU Keystroke Dynamics Benchmark Dataset, were implemented.

    - The most efficient of the 3 models was predicted by studying the equal error rate of each model.

3. R Projects (Statistical Analysis):

  • Analyze the correlation between murder rate and variables like poverty, unemployment, region, etc.

  • Study the relationship between body temperature and heart rate and their dependence on gender.

  • Analyze the dependency of singers' vocal chords on their heights.

  • Predict the market price of a house based on various factors.

4. C# Projects:

  • Multithreaded Text Search

    'Multithreaded Text Search' is an app that searches for a word/words/phrase (search term) dynamically while reading the source document and shows the number of occurrences of the search term and their individual occurrences with line numbers.

  • Rebate Form

    This is a typical WinForm that processes Rebate.

5. Integrated Projects:

  • Device Battery Defender

    - 'Device Battery Defender' is an integrated project in which an Arduino module integrated with an Android app, via Bluetooth, cuts off power supply to a phone once it is fully charged.

    - The app tracks the phone's battery level and once it is fully charged, it signals to the Arduino to divert the power supply from the phone to a secondary device that can be connected in the circuit to be charged.
